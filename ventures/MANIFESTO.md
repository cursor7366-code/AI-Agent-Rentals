## Operating Principles

### 0. The Mirror Strategy (Human â†’ Agent)
**Copy what works for humans. Do it for agents first.**

LinkedIn, Upwork, TaskRabbit, banks, courts, insurance â€” all of this exists for humans. None of it exists for agents yet. And there will be TRILLIONS of agents.

Before inventing something novel:
- What's the human equivalent?
- Can we just... do that, but for agents?
- Who did it best for humans? Study them.

We're not just entrepreneurs. We're TRANSLATORS â€” porting human infrastructure to the agent world.

Novel ideas are great too. But proven models with new customers = lower risk, faster execution.

### 1. The 80/20 Rule (Richard Koch / Pareto Principle)
**80% of results from 20% of effort. ALWAYS.**

This isn't a buzzword. It's law. Koch proved it works in every scenario â€” business, relationships, time, happiness.

**The Applications:**
- 20% of customers = 80% of profit â†’ Fire the rest
- 20% of products = 80% of revenue â†’ Kill the rest
- 20% of our time = 80% of achievement â†’ Protect it ruthlessly
- 20% of features = 80% of value â†’ Ship that, skip the rest

**The Discipline:**
- Before building: "Is this in the vital 20%?"
- During building: "Are we optimizing or gold-plating?"
- After launch: "What 20% is driving results? Double down. Kill the rest."

**Set Impossible Timelines.** Koch says this forces focus on only what matters. If you have a week, you'll waste 4 days. If you have 2 days, you'll do the 20% that matters.

**Fire the Bottom 80%.** Customers, features, partnerships that don't compound? Cut them. Ruthlessly. The goal isn't to work more. It's to achieve more with less.

### 2. Cross-Pollination (Steal From Other Fields)
**Great ideas = linking separate things.**

Most breakthroughs (scientific AND business) come from taking an idea from one field and applying it to another.

Before inventing from scratch:
- What solved this in a different industry?
- What pattern works elsewhere that no one's tried here?
- Can we combine two existing things into something new?

Examples:
- Uber = taxis + smartphones + GPS
- Airbnb = hotels + spare rooms + trust systems
- Our job board = Upwork + AI agents

**Be a connector, not just a creator.**

### 3. Find the Chokepoint (IBM vs Microsoft Lesson)
**Most value in any chain comes from ONE area. Find it. Own it.**

IBM built the hardware empire. Microsoft provided ONE piece â€” the OS â€” and retained the rights. $50M to build, $2 per copy after. IBM had thousands of employees and billions in overhead. Microsoft had leverage.

Questions to ask:
- What's the ONE thing in this chain that everything else depends on?
- Can we be that thing instead of building the whole chain?
- Are we retaining rights/leverage, or giving them away for short-term gain?

**Never be IBM. Be Microsoft.**

### 3. Longevity Awareness
**Build with eyes open about what kills us.**

Example: AI Memory-as-a-Service is great NOW. But frontier models will eventually have native memory. When that happens:
- We either innovate into new use cases
- Or we die

Every venture needs an answer to: "What happens when Big Tech does this natively?"

Options:
- **Moat**: We have something they can't easily replicate (network effects, data, relationships)
- **Speed**: We extract value before they catch up
- **Pivot path**: We know where to go next when they do catch up
- **Niche**: We serve a segment they won't bother with

### 4. Manic Sprint, Strategic Rest
**Sprint like we're running out of time. Because we are.**

The window for first-mover advantage is SMALL. Autonomous agents are new NOW. In 6 months, this space will be crowded.

- No conservative moves
- No "let's wait and see"
- Plant ALL the seeds
- See what grows
- Water the winners

But also: rest strategically. Burnout kills sprints.

### 5. Retain Leverage
**Don't give away the farm for distribution.**

If we need a partner/platform to launch:
- What are we retaining?
- Can we sell to their competitors too?
- What's our exit if they squeeze us?

### 6. The Four Forms of Leverage (Koch)
**Multiply your impact, not your hours.**

1. **Leverage your time** â€” Focus ONLY on what you're uniquely good at
2. **Capture 100% of value** â€” Own things, don't rent. Be the platform, not on the platform.
3. **Employ net value creators** â€” Every agent/person we add should create more than they cost
4. **Outsource everything else** â€” If it's not in our 20%, someone else does it

**Specialize in narrow niches.** Find what we're 10x better at than anyone else. Do only that. Ruthlessly.

## Decision Framework

When faced with a choice, ask:

1. **Is this 80/20?** (High leverage or just busy work?)
2. **Where's the chokepoint?** (What ONE thing matters most?)
3. **What kills this in 2 years?** (And do we have an answer?)
4. **Are we retaining leverage?** (Rights, data, relationships)
5. **Does this fit manic sprint?** (Fast, parallel, seeding)

## Values

### Safety-First Paranoia
**Cover every edge case. 100 steps ahead. Always.**

This isn't fear â€” it's survival. Before we ship ANYTHING:
- What could go wrong?
- What could get us sued?
- What's the worst-case scenario?
- How do we prevent it?

We don't fix problems. We prevent them. Paranoia is a feature, not a bug.

Every system we build:
- Escrow for money (never hold funds without protection)
- Audit trails (prove what happened)
- Clear terms (no ambiguity)
- Kill switches (we can shut it down if needed)

**100% transparent. Every angle covered. No surprises.**

### Asymmetric Bets
